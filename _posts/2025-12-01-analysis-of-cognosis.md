## 1. What was the Cognosis Agent?

### 1-1. A Next-Generation Agent Project That "Makes Decisions and Acts on Its Own"

Cognosis was the name of the **autonomous AI agent project** being tested internally at OpenAI. While the existing ChatGPT was an "AI that responds," the Cognosis agent had the following missions:

- **Understands the user's goals**
- **Plans necessary actions independently**
- **Completes tasks by calling external tools/APIs**
- **Remembers state and automatically executes step-by-step**

In other words, the project aimed to create a true agent that performs **"goal-oriented actions"** without human instruction. This system was being integrated with the o1/o3 research model and was gradually evolving.

### 1-2. Developer-Centered Experiments → Preparation for Expansion into a Fully Open Platform

Initially, Cognosis was only available to an internal testing group and select partners, and was more of a **private R&D project** than a formal feature.

However, with the rapidly evolving AI agent market, OpenAI began refining Cognosis into an independent system by **completely reorganizing its name, structure, and functionality** to expand it into a broader ecosystem.

The result is **Ottogrid**.

---

## 2\. What is Ottogrid?

### 2-1. A New Platform Evolved into an "Agent Operating System"

Ottogrid is more than just a name change. It's more like a new **agent OS (operating stack)** built by OpenAI for the era of AI agents.

The core of Ottogrid is as follows:

- **Autonomous Goal Achievement** — AI automatically performs the necessary actions when the user simply inputs a goal.
- **Tool/Service Connection** — Connects to web APIs, apps, data, and enterprise systems to execute actual tasks.
- **State Management** — Automatically performs not only conversational LLM but also long-term workflows.
- **Multi-step Planning** — AI automation that sequentially executes multi-step procedures.

In other words, Ottogrid is the **core neural network** that connects ChatGPT's "capabilities" to real-world tasks.

### 2-2. Why OpenAI changed its name to Ottogrid

Some possible reasons for the change include:

- **Branding**: Cognosis was the name of a research project, while Ottogrid is a commercially viable name.
- **Ecosystem Scalability**: Evolved into a more comprehensive architecture that considers integration with enterprises, developers, and tools. - **Commercial Direction**: Future integration with the OpenAI Assistants API and MCP (Message Control Protocol) is planned.

Given these trends, Ottogrid is likely to establish itself as the **main brand** of OpenAI's agent strategy.

## 3\. What can Ottogrid actually do?

### 3-1. Smart Automation—A Level Replacement for Zapier and Make

The core of Ottogrid is **“AI-based workflow automation”**.

Example:

> “Create this month's sales report → Import data from Google Sheets → Summarize → Convert to PowerPoint → Share via email.”

Ottogrid will be able to perform this entire process on its own, without human intervention.

### 3-2. Connectivity with External Tools and Enterprise Systems

Ottogrid operates on OpenAI's [Assistants API](https://platform.openai.com/docs/assistants) and [MCP (Message Control Protocol)](https://platform.openai.com/docs/mcp).

This allows for a variety of integrations, including:

- Notion
- Slack
- Google Workspace
- Internal corporate database/API
- Shopping system
- CRM, ERP

In other words, Ottogrid is the **"foundational technology that enables agents to perform actual corporate tasks."**.

### 3-3. Long-Term and Complex Goals

The biggest difference is **"status-based long-term goal execution."**.

For example:

- Automatically reordering when inventory is low
- Report generation and organization every day at 9:00 AM
- Automatically recommending and categorizing email responses
- Long-term project schedule management

These features were previously unavailable with ChatGPT, but are now available with Ottogrid.
