From 2024 to 2026, what will drive the AI industry forward, and which AI models, hardware, and software are currently shaping the market? Let’s explore these questions together.

## 1. Core AI Trends Shaping the Industry from 2024 to 2026

AI is no longer an experimental technology. It is now integrated across industries and evolving into value-centric solutions. In this section, we’ll break down the main trends that will drive AI’s development from 2024 through 2026.

### 1.1. The Rise of Agentic AI Systems

Beyond executing simple commands, **agentic AI systems** are emerging as a key engine of AI progress. These systems can make autonomous decisions, act on them, learn from the outcomes, and manage complex processes within defined boundaries. Over time, they improve themselves and can function like personal assistants in both professional and personal domains.

In **2025**, AI agents are expected to move rapidly from proof-of-concept to real-world deployment across industries, autonomously handling complex, multi-step workflows. By **2026**, adoption of **autonomous AI agents**—systems that don’t just follow instructions but make judgments and execute on their own—is projected to grow sharply. This will significantly increase operational efficiency for organizations and dramatically improve customer experience.

### 1.2. The Evolving Role of Generative AI

Generative AI (GenAI) continues to advance in its ability to generate text, images, code, and even video. The trend points toward GenAI becoming **less visible as a standalone feature**, while being more deeply and seamlessly embedded into services and applications.

In **2024**, generative AI proved its practical value, and domain-specific, customized AI models began to emerge. In **2025**, as baseline LLM capabilities become widely available—often for free—we’re likely to see a rise in **specialized services and applications tailored to specific verticals**, along with broader adoption of open-source LLMs. All of this signals that generative AI will become more sophisticated and will permeate daily life and work in more diverse and subtle ways.

### 1.3. Expansion of Multimodal AI

AI models are increasingly capable of processing and understanding **multiple types of input simultaneously**—text, images, video, audio, and more. This multimodal capability enables more natural, human-like interactions and provides richer situational understanding for tasks such as customer experience, quality control, and data analysis.

In **2025**, AI that can interpret and combine text, images, voice, and video will become more advanced, and we may begin to see new modalities such as **haptic signals or biosignals** being incorporated. By **2026**, multimodal AI is expected to become the **default standard**, meaning models that understand and analyze all forms of data at once will be considered basic AI functionality.

### 1.4. Practical Adoption of SLMs and Edge AI

While large models still attract significant attention, there is growing interest in **smaller, more efficient AI models**—often referred to as **SLMs (Small and Efficient Language Models)**—that can run effectively on local devices such as smartphones and sensors.

This **edge AI** approach offers several advantages: faster decision-making, improved data privacy, reduced bandwidth costs, and lower operating expenses. It also makes AI more practical for real-time applications in sectors like automotive and IoT.

In **2024**, edge AI drew attention for its ability to process data in real time and enable immediate decision-making. In **2025**, **on-device AI**—processing AI locally on personal devices quickly and securely—is expected to receive even more focus.

### 1.5. Proliferation of AI Assistants and Copilots in Everyday Life

AI-based assistants and copilots are reshaping workflows in many domains. Tools like **Microsoft 365 Copilot** and **Google Gemini** are becoming essential interfaces for interacting with software, automating repetitive tasks, and supporting complex operations—from email management and content creation to software development.

New job roles that collaborate with AI—such as AI-assisted reporters, AI strategists, and prompt designers—are emerging, while existing roles are being reorganized. In this environment, the key driver of productivity will shift from traditional skill gaps to **“AI utilization gaps”**—the difference in how effectively individuals can leverage AI.

### 1.6. AI-Driven Innovation in Healthcare and Science

AI is acting as a powerful accelerator in healthcare and scientific research. It speeds up processes like drug discovery, enhances diagnostics through advanced image analysis, and enables personalized medicine based on individual patient data.

Systems like **AlphaFold** for protein structure prediction are already helping accelerate drug development and life science research across the Asia-Pacific region. National-level projects such as the U.S. **“Genesis Mission,”** which aims to radically increase the speed of scientific research using AI, are also noteworthy.

### 1.7. Advances in AI-Powered Automation and Robotics

AI in autonomous systems is expanding far beyond self-driving cars. It now includes **self-managing inventory** in supply chains, **adaptive production lines** in factories, and robots that can observe human behavior and learn new tasks.

In **2025**, commercialization timelines for robots are expected to accelerate significantly, with **humanoid robots** being developed to replace human labor in manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and other sectors. By **2026**, robots are predicted to evolve from “devices” into **“companions”**—seen more like animals than machines.

### 1.8. Deepening Discussions on Ethical AI and Regulation

As AI becomes more deeply integrated into everyday life and business, attention is intensifying around ethical considerations, data privacy, and the development of robust AI governance frameworks. Governments and regulators are actively working to enact laws that ensure responsible AI development and deployment, particularly for high-risk systems.

In **2025**, adherence to AI ethics and trust principles—**transparency, fairness, robustness, privacy, safety, and accountability**—will become a top priority for companies. The importance of establishing related policies and international norms will be further emphasized.

### 1.9. AI for Sustainability

AI is increasingly being used to address environmental challenges—from optimizing energy consumption in data centers and reducing carbon footprints, to predicting crop health for efficient resource use in agriculture and balancing renewable energy grids.

AI is thus becoming a **core tool for a sustainable future**. For example, **Dryad** won the **2025 Rutronik AI Award** for its wildfire early warning system, showcasing how AI can contribute meaningfully to environmental protection.

## 2. AI Products and Services to Watch in November 2025

As of November 2025, there has been striking progress in AI technologies and products, along with a wave of new launches. Key themes include improved model performance, practical AI agents, industry-specific AI solutions, and intensifying competition in AI infrastructure and semiconductors.

### 2.1. Leading AI Models and Assistants

Top-tier AI models are evolving quickly, with major updates from key players.

- **Google Gemini’s Momentum:**  
  Gemini 3 Pro and Gemini 2.5 are drawing significant attention. AI-powered assistance features are deeply integrated across Google Workspace apps like Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Calendar. There have also been reports of a potential partnership with Apple to power the next generation of Siri. Google is using its own AI chips—**Tensor Processing Units (TPUs)**—as core components for Gemini 3, positioning itself as a strong challenger in the AI chip market.
- **OpenAI’s GPT-5.1 Series Opens New Frontiers:**  
  GPT-5.1 **Instant** targets conversational interactions, while GPT-5.1 **Thinking** focuses on efficient reasoning, both generating strong reactions in the market. **GPT-5.1-Codex-Max** is gaining attention as a faster, smarter agentic coding model for complex programming tasks.
- **Anthropic Claude Opus 4.5:**  
  Claude Opus 4.5 delivers advanced capabilities for coding, agent workflows, and computer use, outperforming existing models in these areas. It also shows improved performance in research and spreadsheet-related tasks.

### 2.2. AI Hardware Designed to Boost Productivity

AI is increasingly being embedded into physical products built specifically to enhance productivity.

- **AI Mice:**  
  Traditional mice are evolving into AI assistants with built-in voice recognition and shortcuts that interact with models like ChatGPT. They offer features such as highly accurate speech-to-text conversion and real-time meeting summaries.
- **AI Headphones:**  
  Headphones are moving beyond music playback to provide real-time language translation, adaptive noise cancellation, meeting transcription, and even workout coaching.
- **AI Webcams:**  
  AI-powered webcams automatically optimize users’ appearance and audio to improve virtual interactions. Some models offer 4K resolution and gimbal-like tracking features.
- **AI Tablets:**  
  AI tablets are emerging as powerful, intelligent devices that combine laptop-level performance with built-in voice assistants for screen analysis and AR tools for smart scanning.

### 2.3. Innovative AI Software and Platforms

A wide range of AI-based software and platforms are gaining traction across multiple areas.

- **Creative Design and Content Creation Tools:**  
  Generative AI is rapidly advancing in this category. **Sora** (OpenAI) and **Kling** offer high-consistency text-to-video capabilities, while **Pika** and **Kapwing** enable fast video creation with AI-assisted editing. For image generation, tools like **Canva Magic Design**, **Adobe Firefly**, **Midjourney**, and **Stable Diffusion** remain highly popular.
- **Writing and Content Generation Tools:**  
  Products such as **DeepL** (translation), **QuillBot**, **Jasper AI**, **Anyword**, and **Writesonic** are seeing strong demand.
- **Productivity and Business Solutions:**  
  - **Perplexity AI** serves as a research assistant with transparent citation of sources.  
  - **ClickUp Brain** delivers project-level automation.  
  - **Zapier with AI** and **Make** enable natural language–driven automation across applications.  
  - **Fathom** stands out as an AI meeting recorder that generates accurate transcripts and structured summaries.  
  - **Looka** is an AI-powered logo and brand design tool.  
  - In Korea, AI agents such as TeamSparta’s **Modu AI**, Saltlux’s **Lucia 2** and **Lucia 2.5**, and LG U+’s **Ixio AI Assistant** are also drawing attention.
- **Developer Tools:**  
  - **Microchip MCP Server** provides an AI interface for accessing product data.  
  - **imec.kelis** is an analytical performance modeling tool for designing and optimizing AI data centers.  
  - Google’s **Antigravity** is positioned as an “agent-first IDE” for developers.

### 2.4. Other Notable AI Innovations

- **Dryad:**  
  Winner of the 2025 Rutronik AI Award for its wildfire early warning system.
- **Product Hunt “Best Products of the Week”:**  
  Tools like **Guideflow** (an AI demo automation platform for SaaS) and **Typeless** (an intelligent AI voice dictation tool) have ranked among the top-trending products.
- **Escalating AI Chip Competition:**  
  Soaring AI demand—especially for high-bandwidth memory (HBM)—has deepened AI chip shortages. Analysts are warning that PC and smartphone prices will likely rise by 2026. **TSMC** is building three additional cutting-edge 2nm fabs and plans to begin mass production of 2nm process products in Q4 2025.  
  In response to U.S. sanctions, Chinese companies such as Huawei, with its **Ascend 910C**, and Cambricon, with its **MLU 590**, have successfully developed their own AI chips, reportedly achieving performance levels comparable to NVIDIA’s.
- **AI Infrastructure Build-Out Race:**  
  Companies such as Nokia, which announced a $4 billion AI infrastructure investment plan, exemplify how competition to build AI infrastructure is intensifying.

## 3. Future Outlook: Where AI Is Taking Us

The AI trends and recent product developments we’ve examined clearly show that AI is evolving from a mere tool into an **integrated, indispensable partner** that is reshaping our lives and entire industries. Taken together, the changes from 2024 to 2026 point to AI becoming more intelligent and autonomous, augmenting and extending human capabilities.

In particular, the rise of **agentic AI** and the **standardization of multimodal AI** signal a fundamental shift in how AI understands and processes data. AI systems that can organically combine text, images, voice, and video to form a comprehensive understanding of context—and then make decisions and act on that basis—will not simply automate tasks. They will become **active problem-solvers**, playing a central role in tackling complex challenges. This has the potential to maximize business efficiency, radically boost personal productivity, and help create entirely new services and industry ecosystems.

At the same time, advances in **SLMs and edge AI** are greatly improving access to AI technology. While large cloud-based models provide impressive power, **on-device AI** strengthens privacy and improves real-time responsiveness, enabling more personalized and secure AI experiences. This points toward a future in which AI plays a core role across a wide range of edge devices—including smartphones, wearables, and IoT hardware. Coupled with progress in physical AI, especially robotics, these technologies will drive transformative changes across the real economy, from manufacturing and logistics to healthcare.

However, such rapid progress also demands deep discussion around **ethical AI** and **regulation**. Social consensus and legal frameworks around transparency, fairness, and accountability in AI systems are just as important as technological advancement itself. Given AI’s growing influence on society, it is critical to establish ethical guidelines early in the development process and to continuously work to minimize potential risks.

We must also pay attention to the issue of **“AI-driven productivity polarization.”** If productivity increasingly depends on how effectively individuals and organizations leverage AI, disparities in AI literacy and access could deepen social inequality. That makes it essential to expand education and support so that more people can effectively use AI technologies.

---

In conclusion, AI is not just another wave of technological innovation—it is acting as a **catalyst for fundamental change** across society, the economy, and culture. As many forecasts suggest that **2026** will be a year of consolidation, where AI-centered structures become firmly established, we must both explore AI’s immense potential and thoughtfully address the responsibilities and challenges that come with it.
