# **How AI Became the New Core of Content Creation in 2025: A Deep Dive Into Workflow Transformation, Real Cases, and Future Strategy**

2025 is the year when the phrase **“AI creates content”** finally stopped sounding like an exaggeration. What used to be a niche trend among experimental brands and early adopters has now turned into a global standard. Companies across industries are adopting AI not as an add-on, but as a **central pillar of their marketing and content workflows**.

According to multiple global reports, by 2025 nearly **78% of organizations** use AI in at least one workflow, and **71% are actively using generative AI**. Among all business domains, marketing and content creation show some of the highest adoption rates. These patterns are consistently confirmed in the latest editions of the *McKinsey State of AI* report and in industry-wide adoption statistics.

## **1. Why Everyone Turned to AI for Content in 2025**

The simplest reason AI exploded in content and marketing is that the field demands **speed, scale, personalization, and multi-channel output**, all at once.

If you break down the real workflow of a marketer or creator, you quickly see why AI fits so naturally. Ideation, research, scripting, drafting, visual design, editing, repurposing content for multiple channels, publishing, and analyzing performance—almost every step is built on repetitive cognitive labor.

McKinsey and many other studies highlight the same point: organizations adopt AI primarily for **productivity gains and cost optimization**, and generative AI in particular can handle text, images, code, and data analysis simultaneously. It effectively takes over large portions of “repetitive knowledge work.”

## **2. How AI Has Reshaped the Entire Content Workflow**

In real-world teams, AI is integrated across the **entire content lifecycle**.
For clarity, let’s look at it in four stages:

* Planning (research & strategy)
* Production (text, visuals, video)
* Distribution (multi-channel delivery & automation)
* Analysis (performance, feedback loops)

### **2.1 Planning: AI surfaces what to make before you even start brainstorming**

Traditionally, marketers gathered insights manually—checking Google Trends, competitor campaigns, news, social chatter, and customer reviews one by one. Today, AI tools handle most of this automatically.

Modern AI strategy engines can:

* Analyze search volume trends, keyword clusters, and difficulty
* Scan social platforms (X, Instagram, Reddit) for real-time conversations and sentiment
* Read a brand’s entire content library and identify which topics, lengths, or formats performed well

A particularly instructive example comes from **Zepeto AI**, whose engineering blog explains how AI reviews a brand’s entire content footprint and recommends directions aligned with tone, voice, and past performance.

* Zepeto AI content strategy: [https://zepeto.ai/blog](https://zepeto.ai/blog)
* October 2025 trends: [https://zepeto.ai/blog/2025-october-trends-zepeto-ai-content-strategy](https://zepeto.ai/blog/2025-october-trends-zepeto-ai-content-strategy)

We are shifting away from a world where humans “pull ideas from scratch.”
Now, **AI proposes data-backed opportunities, and humans choose the strategic direction.**

### **2.2 Production: Full-stack generation—text, images, video**

This is the area people feel most directly. AI now drafts:

* Blog posts
* Landing page copy & CTA variants
* Email titles & A/B test versions
* YouTube scripts, Instagram captions, TikTok dialogue

And with brand-tone guidelines provided, AI can maintain surprisingly consistent voice.

Visual production has transformed even more. Major industries—travel, fashion, gaming, consumer electronics—now deploy AI to generate promo images, B-roll, thumbnails, and short campaigns.

A great example is **TUI**, the large European travel company.
They use generative AI to create **personalized travel mood videos** and partner with AI-trip platform **Mindtrip** to convert AI-generated itineraries into bookable packages.

* TUI × Mindtrip partnership:
  [https://www.tuigroup.com/en/newsroom/news/tui-cooperation-with-mindtrip-ai-travel-inspiration-becomes-a-bookable-reality](https://www.tuigroup.com/en/newsroom/news/tui-cooperation-with-mindtrip-ai-travel-inspiration-becomes-a-bookable-reality)

Brands increasingly operate with this workflow:
AI generates *many* versions → humans select → humans refine.

### **2.3 Distribution: automation for “where, when, and how to publish”**

Knowing **what** to publish is step one. Knowing **where and how often** is step two—and AI excels here as well.

AI systems now:

* Recommend posting schedules
* Rewrite content for each platform’s style
* Auto-adjust frequency based on real-time engagement
* For ads, generate **hundreds** of creative combinations and phase out poor performers automatically

A new concept emerging in 2025 is **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)**:
Optimizing content not just for Google SEO, but for *AI engines* like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, etc.

TUI, again, is an early leader here—designing content so that **AI travel agents naturally recommend TUI packages inside conversations**.

* GEO insight (TUI):
  [https://ie.shafaqna.com/EN/AL/3243442](https://ie.shafaqna.com/EN/AL/3243442)

### **2.4 Analysis: AI interprets performance across all channels**

Finally, analytics—the part marketers used to dread the most.

Instead of opening ads managers, analytics dashboards, and CRM exports one by one, AI now:

* Aggregates metrics across channels
* Identifies which formats, tones, titles, or designs performed best
* Summarizes insights in natural language
* Suggests what to double down on next month

This shift frees marketers from “data wrangling” and lets them focus on **strategic decisions, not spreadsheets.**

## **3. Real Cases: When AI Helps—and When It Backfires**

### **3.1 TUI: AI inspiration videos + GEO = a new kind of sales funnel**

TUI’s approach shows how AI content can directly influence revenue.
By generating personalized travel mood videos and blending them into bookable itineraries, TUI created a **new bridge between inspiration and purchase**.

Their GEO strategy ensures that when people ask AI assistants for travel ideas, TUI’s offerings appear organically in the conversation.

### **3.2 Zepeto AI: The Korean model of using AI as a “content teammate”**

Zepeto AI has become a strong reference point in Korea. Their approach—training AI on a brand’s entire content footprint—positions AI as a consistent **assistant-level content partner**.

AI handles repetitive drafting and editing.
Humans handle tone, priorities, and cultural nuance.
This hybrid model is increasingly becoming the Asian standard.

### **3.3 McDonald’s Netherlands: A cautionary tale of emotional mismatch**

On the other end, we have a major failure case.
McDonald’s Netherlands released an AI-generated Christmas ad that viewers found:

* Cold
* Depressing
* Out of touch with holiday sentiment

It sparked widespread backlash, leading the brand to remove the ad and issue a public apology.

* Article:
  [https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/dec/11/mcdonalds-removes-ai-generated-christmas-ad-advert-backlash](https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/dec/11/mcdonalds-removes-ai-generated-christmas-ad-advert-backlash)

The core lesson:
**Technical execution is no longer the hard part. Emotional resonance is.**
AI can produce content quickly, but it doesn’t intuitively understand cultural warmth or timing—and brands must evaluate these aspects carefully.

## **4. Strengths and Risks: The Double-Edged Nature of AI Content**

### **Strengths**

* Massive productivity gains
* Easy multi-language, multi-format production
* Hyper-personalized messaging
* Faster insight loops and strategic adjustments

Many studies estimate **26–55% productivity improvements** in teams that integrate AI effectively.

### **Risks**

1. **Data bias & quality problems**
   AI can reinforce harmful stereotypes if training data is skewed.

2. **Regulation & transparency**
   Governments are beginning to require disclosure for AI-generated ads and media.

3. **Brand trust & emotional nuance**
   AI may miss subtle cultural or emotional signals.

4. **Lack of human review**
   McKinsey reports that only **27% of teams review all AI outputs** before publishing.
   This opens clear risk areas.

* McKinsey PDF:
  [https://www.mckinsey.com/~/media/mckinsey/.../the-state-of-ai-how-organizations-are-rewiring-to-capture-value_final.pdf](https://www.mckinsey.com/~/media/mckinsey/.../the-state-of-ai-how-organizations-are-rewiring-to-capture-value_final.pdf)

Ultimately, AI is a powerful tool—not an autonomous teammate—and still requires structured oversight.

## **5. The Path Forward: Treat AI as a Teammate, but Keep Humans in Charge**

For individual creators, bloggers, and small brands, the implications are clear:

* **AI adoption is now essential, not optional.**
* But relying on AI entirely is dangerous. Emotional and cultural judgment still require human intuition.
* The winning strategy is a **division of labor**:

### **AI handles:**

* Drafting at scale
* Summaries, rewrites, translations
* Pattern discovery in content performance

### **Humans handle:**

* Direction, values, and brand narrative
* Final quality review
* Ethical, cultural, and emotional alignment

A final point:
We are moving toward an era where users increasingly **encounter content inside AI systems**—not search engines.
So visibility inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other generative platforms will become a new competitive frontier.

In short, the role of creators is shifting from “people who type” to **“people who direct.”**

AI-powered content marketing is no longer a trend—it is the new infrastructure.
The real question of 2025 is not *if* you will use AI, but **how you will design it into your workflow to become your advantage.**
